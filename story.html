The romanization problem — why your map is lying to you

Japanese restaurant names aren't text. They're cultural artifacts: classical Chinese readings, irregular place-name pronunciations, katakana loan-words that must be back-translated to English, historical terms that predate modern Japanese. Standard romanization libraries treat them as text — and the results aren't subtle errors. They're completely different words.

This isn't transliteration. It's cultural intelligence at inference speed.

We didn't trust one AI. We ran two.

We evaluated 53,502 restaurant records independently with Claude Sonnet 5 and Claude Opus 4.8 — same dataset, same prompt, zero collaboration — then compared every verdict (53,502 total records evaluated; 51,967 shared between both models — the remainder were ASCII names or override-excluded records).

Where the data comes from

Three independent layers, cross-checked into one clean, consistent schema — no single source we lean on, and nothing we can't stand behind.

  • Web-observed facts — the base layer: shop names, addresses and locations, observed from public ramen listings on the web. Re-checked every week — this is what powers our closure-candidate detection. Factual data; no third-party copyright asserted.
  • Municipal open data — official records published by Japanese local governments through their open-data portals, including food-business permits: openings, closures, address changes. © 各自治体オープンデータ · CC BY.
  • Our own enrichment — geocoding, romanization, keito (broth-style) classification and the nearest station, all generated by us. © gachi-tokusuru.com.

The value isn't any single source — it's the cross-checking, plus the 26,975 romanization fixes on top and a weekly liveness pass that keeps the set honest about what's still open. Every Monday each shop's last-seen date is refreshed, anything gone missing two weeks running is flagged and double-checked before we drop it, and a weekly change feed records every opening and closure. Anyone can sell you a list; almost no one keeps re-proving it's still standing.

Coordinates — the pin has to be right

Clean names aren't enough; a pin on the wrong block breaks the experience. Every one of the 62,173 records carries a latitude & longitude — geocoded from its address, not inherited from a scrape.

We actively purge bad coordinates. When we found 131 overseas branches a Japan-only geocoder had silently parked on garbage domestic coordinates (a Hong Kong shop sitting in Niigata), we detected and removed every one. Each shop is then enriched with its nearest station — name, English name, and distance in metres.

The Ramen Shop atlas — reading culture off the map

Ramen Shop (ラーメンショップ, "Ra-sho") is a Kanto-born, franchise-by-apprenticeship chain — the roadside, free-parking, open-late kind. There are 374 nationwide, and their distribution is a map of Japanese car culture itself.

Tokyo has fewer than half of what its own suburbs do. Ra-sho runs on roadside parking and arterial highways — a poor fit for expensive, train-first central Tokyo, where the 22 shops cluster in the western Tama sprawl and the outer rim of the 23 wards. Car-society vs. train-society, read straight off the map.

Kyushu is a near-blank: 5 shops across 7 prefectures. In the tonkotsu heartland, the Kanto transplant never took. And then the anomaly: an enclave of 13 in Ehime, on Shikoku — anchored by "Ramen Shop KANTO Chain · West-Shikoku HQ" in Imabari, a Kanto brand that put down roots 1,000 km from home and kept the name "KANTO."

Where we're honest about the data. Name-matching finds every shop called ラーメンショップ — but it can't separate the true apprenticeship-lineage shops (the Tsubaki line: 23 of the 374) from independents that just borrowed the name (Kita-ku's "Old Ramen Shop Gyakuryu," Itabashi's "Ramen Shop Pretty Bear"). Telling those apart needs human domain knowledge — a future enrichment layer. We'd rather show you exactly what name-matching can and can't tell you.

Run this slice yourself: GET /v1/shops?q=ラーメンショップ&pref=Ehime — or over MCP, search_ramen(q="ラーメンショップ", pref="愛媛県"). Counts as of July 2026.
